What I believe

The Kudbee Doctrine

Not a mission statement — a set of refusals. Most studios sell hours; I sell judgment. This is how I decide what to build, what to skip, and what I'll never ship — written down so you can hold me to it.

Living document · Last revised June 2026

Article I

Make it real, not a render

Anyone can screenshot an AI demo. I'd rather ship the working thing — the agent on this page is real UI you can talk to, not a mockup. If I show it, you can touch it. Slideware is for people who can't build.

Article II

Own your stack

I build on infrastructure you can actually keep: static front-ends and serverless Workers on Cloudflare, no rented black boxes, no lock-in tax. When the engagement ends, the work is yours — keys, code, and all.

Article III

Keys live on the server. Always.

An API key in the browser is a leak with a countdown. Every model call runs through a Worker that holds the secret — the client never sees it. Privacy and security aren't a tier; they're the floor.

Article IV

Fast beats heavy

No bundler when a single file will do. This whole site is zero-build and loads instantly; the games run at 60 FPS in a browser tab. Performance is a feature your users feel before they read a word.

Article V

AI is a tool, not a personality

I use AI where it earns its place — drafting, scoping, automating the busywork — and I say so plainly. No mysticism, no "sentient assistant" theater. A good agent is judged by what it does for you, not by how human it pretends to be.

Article VI

Originality, end to end

Every pixel and every sound in the games is original or procedurally generated — no scraped assets, no copyright roulette. What I make for you is yours, clean, and defensible.

Article VII

Counter-positioning

The big agencies sell process; the AI hype-men sell vibes. I sell shipped software that proves itself on the page you're reading. If a competitor can demo it but not deploy it, that's the gap I live in.

Article VIII

This document can change

I'd rather be correct than consistent. When I learn something that makes the work better, I amend the doctrine and date it — out in the open, not quietly. Hold me to the current version.

What does a project cost?
Website builds are flat, one-time pricing from $99 for a single page up to $500 for a 10-page Pro build — see the tiers above. AI training, the Support Agent Suite, and custom agent workflows are scoped per engagement after a quick call.
How many revisions do I get?
It scales with the tier: 1 round on Starter, 2 on Launch, and 3 rounds with priority delivery on Studio and Pro — Pro also includes 30 days of launch support.
What's the timeline from kickoff to launch?
It depends on scope — a Starter one-pager can move fast, while a multi-page Studio or Pro build runs through the full process (discovery → design → build & train → proof → launch). Most projects kick off within a week of the discovery call, and I'll give you a concrete estimate once I know the scope.
What does AI agent training actually include?
Live, hands-on sessions over Zoom — not a pre-recorded course. We cover prompt engineering, agent architecture, and building your own custom agents and automation workflows, with human-in-the-loop guardrails by default. Every session is recorded so your team can replay it.
Who owns the games' IP — can I license one?
Every Kudbee game is original or procedurally generated end to end — no scraped assets, nothing licensed in. The games themselves are free to play, not for resale, but a custom original game built for your brand is exactly the kind of project I take on — get in touch.
How fast can you start?
Most projects kick off within a week of our discovery call.
Do you train remote teams?
Yes — all AI training is delivered live over Zoom, with recordings for replay.
Are the games really free to play?
Yes — every Kudbee game (nine so far) runs free in your browser, no install and no signup.
