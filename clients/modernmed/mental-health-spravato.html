The basics

What SPRAVATO (esketamine) is.

An FDA-approved option for depression that hasn't responded to other treatments.

SPRAVATO is the brand name for esketamine, an FDA-approved prescription nasal spray for treatment-resistant depression in adults. It works differently from standard antidepressants, which is part of why it can help when several other medications have not.

Because of how esketamine can affect blood pressure, alertness, and perception in the hours after a dose, it is not a take-home medication. SPRAVATO is self-administered in a certified medical setting under supervision, with a monitoring period afterward, and is taken alongside an oral antidepressant. These safeguards are part of its FDA REMS (Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy) program. Because you may feel drowsy or unsettled, you'll need someone to drive you home.

SPRAVATO is given in-office under medical monitoring per its REMS program — never on your own at home — so safety is built into every session.

How treatment works.

A clear, monitored path — with a physician alongside you at every step.

1

Confidential consult

A private conversation about your history, the treatments you've tried, and your goals. Your physician reviews whether SPRAVATO or another approach is the right fit — no obligation, no judgment.

2

Eligibility & plan

If SPRAVATO is appropriate, your physician explains the REMS requirements, coordinates an oral antidepressant where needed, and sets a treatment schedule with you before you begin.

3

In-office sessions

You self-administer the SPRAVATO nasal spray in the office under supervision. Sessions are calm and unhurried, and you'll arrange a ride home in advance.

4

Monitoring period

After each dose you're monitored on-site for the period required by the REMS program, so any effects are observed and managed before you head home.

5

Ongoing plan

Your physician tracks your response and adjusts the plan over time — combining SPRAVATO, medication management, and psychotherapy into one coordinated course of care.

Questions

SPRAVATO & mental health, answered.

What is SPRAVATO (esketamine)?

SPRAVATO is the brand name for esketamine, an FDA-approved prescription nasal spray for treatment-resistant depression in adults. It is taken in a certified healthcare setting under medical monitoring, alongside an oral antidepressant, as part of its REMS safety program.

Who is SPRAVATO for?

SPRAVATO is intended for adults with treatment-resistant depression — depression that hasn't responded adequately to other antidepressants. Eligibility is determined during a psychiatric consult, where your physician reviews your history and current treatment to see whether it's an appropriate option for you.

Why is SPRAVATO given in the office?

Because of how esketamine can affect blood pressure, alertness, and perception shortly after a dose, SPRAVATO must be self-administered in a certified setting under supervision, with a monitoring period afterward. This in-office requirement is part of its FDA REMS program, and you'll need someone to drive you home.

Do you offer other mental health services?

Yes. Alongside SPRAVATO, the practice offers psychiatric medication management and psychotherapy, so depression and related concerns can be treated within one coordinated plan rather than across disconnected providers.

Does insurance cover SPRAVATO and mental health care?

What should I do if I'm in crisis?
