## The wedge (why we can win)
ModernMed runs **three overlapping domains** — `modernmedchicago.com`, `modernmedrecovery.com`,
`modernmedconcierge.co` — on WordPress, with some pages returning **503/404**. That fragmentation
is their biggest weakness: duplicate/near-duplicate content across domains makes Google **pick one
version and filter the rest**, splitting links and authority three ways instead of compounding it.
A single, reliable, fast, schema-rich, E-E-A-T-strong site (which we're already building) plus a
dialed-in Google Business Profile and a real review engine **outranks a split, unreliable footprint.**

---

## TIER 0 — The foundational move (do this first)
1. **Consolidate 3 domains → 1 canonical.** 301-redirect `modernmedrecovery.com` and
   `modernmedconcierge.co` into one primary domain. This is **Google's explicitly preferred**
   consolidation method and concentrates all link equity/signals into one URL set.
   **Impact: very high · Effort: medium.** *(Verified — Google docs.)*
2. **Kill the 503/404 reliability problem.** Crawl all three sites, map every error/duplicate, and
   migrate to one reliable host. A site that's always up and fast is an instant edge.
   **Impact: high · Effort: medium.** *(Needs a live crawl — Screaming Frog — to list exact URLs.)*

## TIER 1 — Quick wins (high impact, low effort)
3. **Google Business Profile (GBP):** claim/verify **ONE** complete profile for the Park Ridge office —
   full categories, services, hours, photos, weekly Posts, Q&A. GBP is the single biggest local-pack
   lever. **Impact: very high · Effort: low.** *(Verified — top local factor.)*
4. **NAP consistency** (Name/Address/Phone identical across the site + Yelp, Healthgrades, Psychology
   Today, Doximity). **Find and remove any duplicate GBP listings** (they split reviews and risk
   suspension). **Impact: high · Effort: low.** *(Verified.)*
   - *Note: trivial formatting like "Ste 202" vs "Suite 202" is normalized by Google — don't over-worry it.*
5. **Review engine:** steady, **authentic** review growth (a post-visit ask flow), not spikes. 80%+ of
   patients read reviews before booking. **Impact: very high · Effort: low-medium.** *(Verified.)*
6. **Per-page titles + meta descriptions** targeting real intent (replace generic/duplicated tags).
   **Impact: medium · Effort: low.**
7. **Structured data / schema** on every page: `Physician` (Dr. Oyasu), `MedicalClinic`/`LocalBusiness`,
   and per service `MedicalProcedure` / `MedicalTherapy` / `MedicalTest`, plus `FAQPage`.
   **Impact: medium-high · Effort: low-medium.** *(Verified — note "MedicalServices" is not a real type.)*

## TIER 2 — Content architecture (the moat)
8. **Deep, dedicated service pages — one per line**, each with its own URL, schema, FAQs, and
   cited/referenced copy:
   - Medication-Assisted Recovery / **Suboxone** (lean hardest here — highest intent + their origin line)
   - **Testosterone / TRT & men's health**
   - **Medical weight loss / GLP-1 (semaglutide)**
   - **SPRAVATO / mental health**
   **Impact: high · Effort: high.**
9. **Condition / education pages** with `MedicalCondition` schema (symptoms, treatments, risk factors,
   tests) — e.g., opioid use disorder, low testosterone — to win informational + "near me" intent and
   build **topical authority**. **Impact: high · Effort: high.**
10. **Local "near me" / suburb pages** done well (Park Ridge, Niles, Des Plaines, Edison Park, NW Chicago)
    — genuine local content, not doorway spam. **Impact: medium-high · Effort: medium.**
11. **E-E-A-T (critical for YMYL medical):** prominent Dr. Oyasu authorship + board certifications,
    "medically reviewed by," references/citations, real trust pages. Google applies its strictest
    trust standards to medical content. **Impact: high · Effort: medium.** *(Verified.)*

## TIER 4 — Backlinks & authority (ongoing)
12. **Citations/directories:** optimized profiles on medical + local directories (Healthgrades,
    Psychology Today, Doximity, FindATopDoc, chamber of commerce, local business listings).
13. **Digital PR / expert positioning:** Dr. Oyasu already has a TopDoctor interview — build on it with
    HARO/Qwoted responses and local press on the opioid crisis / men's health. **High authority value.**
14. **Linkable assets** (these earn links *and* convert): the "First 72 Hours" induction guide, a COWS

15. **Partnership links:** gyms, sober-living homes, therapists, men's-health brands → referral pages.

## TIER 5 — Measurement & compliance (set up correctly from day one)
16. **HIPAA-aware analytics — do NOT bolt on standard Google Analytics / Meta Pixel to medical pages.**
    Google offers **no BAA** for GA and bars its use on HIPAA-covered pages; a cookie banner is **not**
    valid HIPAA authorization. Use **BAA-covered / server-side** analytics (Piwik PRO with BAA, Matomo
    on-prem, or server-side GTM with PHI scrubbing). **Impact: compliance-critical.** *(Verified — HHS + Google.)*
17. **KPIs to track:** local-pack rankings, GBP calls / direction requests / clicks, organic consult-form
    submissions and calls, keyword rankings, Core Web Vitals. *(This is exactly what the Kudbee
    dashboard's Growth tab is built to show.)*
18. **Wire up:** Google Search Console, GBP Insights, a rank tracker, and Ahrefs/SEMrush for the
    competitive/keyword data the items above flagged as estimates.
