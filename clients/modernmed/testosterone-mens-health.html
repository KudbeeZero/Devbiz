The basics

What testosterone optimization is.

Hormone care that starts with your labs, not a sales pitch.

Testosterone is the primary male hormone, and it naturally shifts with age and health. When levels fall low enough to cause symptoms — a condition clinicians call low testosterone (or hypogonadism) — men often notice low energy, reduced libido, trouble with focus and mood, and a loss of muscle or strength.

Testosterone replacement therapy, or TRT, restores testosterone toward a healthy range when symptoms are paired with confirmed bloodwork. Done properly, it is a well-established treatment — but it only belongs with the right diagnosis, a personalized plan, and ongoing monitoring. This page describes hormone optimization in general terms; whether it fits you is a decision your physician makes with you.

TRT is a medical treatment for a measurable condition. It begins with labs and a diagnosis — not with a guess.

Questions

Testosterone & men's health, answered.

What are the symptoms of low testosterone?

Common symptoms men describe include low energy or fatigue, reduced libido, trouble with focus or mood, and loss of muscle or strength. These symptoms overlap with many other conditions, so testosterone therapy is considered only when symptoms are paired with confirmed bloodwork and a physician's evaluation.

How do I know if TRT is right for me?

TRT is appropriate for adult men who have both symptoms of low testosterone and lab values that confirm it. The process starts with baseline bloodwork and a confidential consult, and your physician decides whether a personalized plan makes sense for you.

Is testosterone therapy safe?

When it's individualized and physician-supervised, testosterone therapy is a well-established treatment. Safety comes from doing it properly: confirming the diagnosis with bloodwork, tailoring the plan to you, and monitoring labs over time to keep treatment on track.
